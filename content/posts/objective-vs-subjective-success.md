This will probably take more of a rambling approach than most of my writing to
date, especially since it is entirely non-technical.

Recently, mostly today, I've been thinking a lot more about measures of success
and trying to frame how I'm doing. I keep running into the gap between
objective and subjective success, which makes me think more negatively than I
perhaps should.

## Comparison struggles

It's worth starting this out by saying that I have a comparison problem, in
both directions.

Frequently, people say that they spend too much time comparing themselves to
others, with the phrase "comparison is the thief of joy" being touted. I'm
definitely guilty of this, for every person you meet there will be something
they have that you want. It might be a job that pays more money, a more
aesthetic physique or a character trait that you envy.

At the same time, I find that a lack of comparison will also cause you
problems. If you only ever look at what people have over you, it'll be
difficult to see what you have over them and to be grateful for that.

Even more so, it becomes difficult to look at the wider population as a whole
and understand where you sit, especially if you have a habit of comparing
yourself only to the best.

The latter is likely where I struggle the most, always feeling like there is so
much improvement to be had.

## Objective success

When we look at societal measures, I am objectively very successful.

Professionally, I have a job that pays well, I love doing and affords me plenty
of time for myself. It doesn't consume me, the people I work with are fantastic
and I often don't have a preference between weekdays and weekends. It feels
weird to say (since I rarely tout my achievements), but I'm also really good at
what I do. Our company attracts high performing engineers and I'm at the top of
that bracket in a lot of metrics.

Physically, I'm well trained and able-bodied. I don't have any injuries that
prevent me from doing what I want to do on a day-to-day basis, and
aesthetically I'm likely in the top 1% of people if you consider the entire
population.

Mentally, I'm highly educated and operate extremely autonomously. I've got a
good understanding of myself and what I stand for these days. I have strong
discipline, and I'd like to think I'm reasonably emotionally intelligent. I
plan a lot for the future and consider where and who I would like to be, trying
to make changes early in order to make as much progress as possible before it's
required.

Financially, I'm probably doing quite well, I've got a lot of money saved up
and based on recent changes my investments and financial decisions should set
me up well for the future.
